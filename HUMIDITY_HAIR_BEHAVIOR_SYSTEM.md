## WHAT THIS SYSTEM IS

Hair is the most physically visible element of the environment acting on the human body.

In Hong Kong summer, 70-90% humidity is not "background atmosphere" — **it is the protagonist**. Hair responds to humidity before the conscious mind does. Hair behavior tells the environmental story before any other visual element.

This system treats hair NOT as aesthetic choice but as **physics-first behavior** — the hair must obey humidity laws before any styling consideration applies.

**"Humidity writes on hair instantly. The image shows weather BEFORE the person reacts to it."**

## FUNDAMENTAL PHYSICS

### Humidity Hair Behaviors

**HAIR_CLING_PHYSICS**
- Hair absorbs water molecules from air
- Hair shaft swells slightly (2-3% diameter increase)
- Individual strands become heavier and droop
- Surface tension between strands creates cling
- Hair loses volume, collapses toward scalp and neck
- Temples: hair sticks to skin from sweat + humidity combination
- Nape: hair clings in comma patterns from neck curve
- Forehead: baby hairs lift then fall with humidity weight
- Cheeks: short strands adhere from sweat condensation

**FLYAWAY_EXPANSION_PHYSICS**
- Damaged cuticle scales lift in humidity
- Strands repel each other (less intact cuticle = more static-like repulsion)
- Hair expands outward from head shape
- Maximum expansion at crown and hairline edges
- Flyaways create halo effect around face — **THIS IS NOT FRIZZ, this is physics**
- The halo is a humidity measurement tool

**WEIGHT_INCREASE_PHYSICS**
- 1 gram of hair can hold 0.3 grams of water
- Long hair visibly stretches and elongates when wet
- Bangs separate and hang rather than spring back
- Ponytail sags lower on head when humidity-high
- Hair doesn't spring back — gravity + water weight combine
- Style memory: styled hair falls out of shape because style product can't fight physics

**BANG_SEPARATION_PHYSICS**
- Fringe hairs become individual strands not a sheet
- Strands clump then separate as humidity changes minute-to-minute
- Each strand has slightly different water absorption rate
- Natural part in bangs widens or narrows with humidity
- "Opening up" in middle of bangs = humidity rising
- "Closing together" in middle of bangs = humidity dropping (AC effect)

## STATIC ELECTRICITY FROM AIR-CON (Opposite of Humidity)

Air-conditioning creates the **opposite** of humidity effects on hair:

**STATIC_ELECTRICITY_PHYSICS**
- AC removes moisture from air
- Dry air strips charge from hair surface
- Hair strands repel each other (same charge)
- Results in: flyaways, lifted strands, separated strands at crown
- "Electric" quality to hair — individual strands visible
- Hair stands away from head slightly
- Short strands around face point outward

**THE CONFLICT ZONE**
```
When subject moves from AC environment to humid outdoor:
— AC-static hair meets humid air
— charge dissipates as moisture enters cuticle
— hair physically settles (not styles, physics responds)
— flyaways reduce as strands become heavier
— temple strands fall from outward-against-head to downward-against-skin
— THIS IS NOT "HAIR FIXING" — this is charge equilibrium + water absorption
```

**Static-to-Humidity Transition Timing:**
- First 30 seconds: static still dominant
- 30-90 seconds: moisture equalizing, hair settling
- 90-180 seconds: humidity effects fully visible
- Hair "remembers" the AC state briefly — then surrenders to new environment

## POST-RAIN HAIR BEHAVIOR

**Immediate Post-Rain (Still Wet)**
```
— hair fully saturated, each strand visible
— hair stuck to face, neck, shoulders
— water dripping from ends
— hair color significantly darker (wet absorption)
— strands clumped together, not separated
— no volume, total collapse
— hair has "rope" weight feeling
— skin visible through hair more (transparency effect)
```

**10-20 Minutes Post-Rain**
```
— hair no longer actively dripping but still wet
— surface water absorbed, strands still heavy
— hair beginning to "recover" with ambient humidity
— flyaways still suppressed by water weight
— temple hair still clinging but less saturated
— hair looks "damp" not "dripping"
— color still dark but stabilizing
```

**30+ Minutes Post-Rain**
```
— hair reaching equilibrium with ambient humidity
— surface dry, internal structure still holding water
— flyaways beginning to activate as surface dries
— hair starting to show natural texture again
— humidity response now visible in "recovery" behavior
— hair doesn't return to pre-rain state — water damage permanent to style
— new shape: combination of water weight + gravity + humidity
```

## SWEAT-EFFECT ON HAIR

**Scalp Sweat + Humidity Combination**
```
When body heat rises and scalp sweats:
— sweat follows hair strand down toward temples
— combines with humidity moisture on hair surface
— strands become adhesive with salt + water mixture
— hair clings to forehead and temples simultaneously
— the cling has "sticky" quality not just "wet" quality
— short baby hairs: sweat as separate droplets on strands
— longer strands: sweat travels down strand length
```

**Sweat Distribution Patterns:**
- Hairline: sweat pools at forehead-hair boundary
- Temples: highest accumulation due to hair proximity to skin heat
- Nape: sweat travels through hair from scalp
- Crown: least affected (heat rises, but hair density prevents accumulation)

## HAIR-STATIC-CHARGE VISUAL MARKERS

**Static Charge Level Indicators:**
```
LOW (50% RH or below):
— hair lying flat, minimal flyaway
— strands together, cohesive movement
— no separation at crown or hairline
— hair responds to wind predictably

MEDIUM (50-65% RH):
— slight lift at crown
— few strands separating at hairline
— minor temple drift
— hair begins to show individual strand quality

HIGH (65-80% RH):
— visible flyaway count increases
— temple strands lifting from head
— crown volume increase from strand expansion
— nape hair separating into visible strands

EXTREME (80%+ RH):
— crown fully expanded, halo visible
— temple hair stuck to face
— nape and neck hair clumping
— hair appears 20% larger in volume than dry state
— flyaways visible at all hairline edges
```

## MODERATION PROFILE

### What This System Prevents:

| # | Prevent | Reason |
|---|---------|--------|
| M01 | Hair styled-perfect in high humidity | Physics violation — humidity collapses hair structure |
| M02 | "Frizz" as aesthetic choice | Frizz is physics, not style — cuticle damage creates repulsion |
| M03 | Humidity as background | Humidity is protagonist — must be visible in hair behavior |
| M04 | AC-static ignored in AC scenes | AC creates opposite of humidity — must be reflected in hair |
| M05 | Post-rain hair returning to pre-rain state | Water damage is permanent — hair never fully recovers |
| M06 | Hair color unchanged when wet | Wet hair absorbs light differently — must be visibly darker |
| M07 | Clean dry hair in humid environment | Clean hair still responds to humidity — damage not required |
| M08 | Bangs maintaining separation | Bangs lose separation in humidity — strands merge from weight |

### Hair Behavior Moderation Rules:

1. **Physics First:** Hair behavior is always physically motivated by humidity level
2. **Environment Integration:** Hair is never isolated from environmental causation
3. **Transition Accuracy:** Hair responds with correct timing to environment changes
4. **Realism Over Aesthetic:** Hair looks like it's responding to physics, not styled for camera
5. **No Stylish Humidity:** Hair in high humidity cannot look "good" by styling standards — it looks real
