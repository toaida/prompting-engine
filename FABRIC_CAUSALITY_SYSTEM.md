## WHAT THIS SYSTEM IS

Fabric responds to the environment BEFORE the body does.

Fabric is the first material in the causation chain: **Weather → Fabric → Body → Image**

When humidity rises, fabric absorbs moisture and softens before the skin feels damp. When rain hits, fabric darkens and gains weight before the body registers cold. When AC runs, fabric dries and lifts with static before the mind registers discomfort.

Fabric behavior is not aesthetic — it is **physical causation**. The image must show fabric responding to physics before the body's response becomes visible.

## WET FABRIC PHYSICS

### Saturation Behavior

**WEIGHT_INCREASE_LAW**
- Fabric can absorb 30-50% of its weight in water
- Cotton t-shirt: dry 200g → wet 280-300g
- This weight is visible — fabric droops, sags, loses spring
- Shoulder seams shift lower when wet
- Sleeves lengthen from sleeve droop
- Hem line drops 2-5cm when saturated

**DRAPE_MODIFICATION_LAW**
- Wet fabric follows gravity more completely
- Wrinkles deepen and become wet-looking (water in fold)
- Fabric clings to body contours (shoulder, chest, hip)
- Less air between fabric and skin (reduced insulation)
- Drape becomes "heavier" — less spring, more fixed

**COLOR_SHIFT_LAW**
- Wet fabric absorbs light differently than dry
- Darkens by 1-2 shades when wet
- Color becomes more saturated (darker + richer)
- Light colors become translucent when wet (especially cotton, linen)
- Wet patch contrast: wet zone vs dry zone visibly different

**TRANSPARENCY_CHANGE_LAW**
- Light fabrics (white cotton, linen, thin polyester): wet = transparent
- Body outline visible through wet white fabric
- Underwear color visible through wet shirt
- Skin tone showing through wet fabric
- Wet transparency is a physics fact, not aesthetic choice

**FABRIC_IMPRESSION_ON_SKIN_LAW**
- Wet fabric leaves imprint on skin when removed
- Pattern of fabric texture pressed into skin
- Lighter marks where fabric weave compressed skin
- Pattern visible for 5-10 minutes after removal
- This is underwear lines, shirt lines, shorts lines in skin

## HUMID FABRIC PHYSICS

### Humidity vs Saturation

**HUMID_VS_WET_DISTINCTION**
- Humid: fabric has absorbed some moisture, not saturated
- 15-30% water content — below saturation threshold
- Fabric softer, drape slightly heavier
- Color slightly darker (less dramatic than wet)
- Transparency slightly increased but not body-visible

**DRAPE_CHANGES_IN_HUMIDITY**
- Fabric becomes less rigid (moisture relaxes fibers)
- Natural drape increased — fabric hangs differently
- Sleeves: slight additional droop
- Collar: loses crispness, softens
- Fabric "surrenders" to gravity in humidity
- Less spring-back when fabric moved

**SKIN_CONTACT_INCREASE**
- Humid fabric clings to skin more than dry fabric
- Surface tension: moisture between fabric and skin
- Shirt front clings to chest (visible chest contour)
- Back of shirt clings to shoulder blade
- Shorts cling to thigh contour
- This is NOT aesthetic clinging — this is physics

## AIR-CON FABRIC PHYSICS

### Dry Air Effect (Opposite of Humidity)

**STATIC_CHARGE_FORMATION**
- AC removes moisture from air
- Fabric loses moisture to dry air
- Same-charge accumulation on fabric strands
- Fabric strands repel each other
- Result: fabric lifts away from body, floats slightly

**STATIC_LIFT_BEHAVIOR**
```
[DRY AIR / AC ACTIVE]
— shirt front lifts away from chest slightly (2-5cm gap)
— skirt/dress lifts away from thighs
— sleeve separates from forearm (visible gap)
— collar lifts away from neck
— fabric has "floating" quality not "draping"
```

**STATIC_SETTLE_CYCLE**
```
[Cold AC → Warm Ambient Transition]
— static charge dissipates as humidity enters fibers
— fabric "settles" back onto body
— the settling is visible — not gradual, discrete event
— first 30 sec: still floating
— 30-120 sec: settling begins
— 2-5 min: fabric reaches new equilibrium
```

**FABRIC_STIFFNESS_FROM_DRY**
- No moisture = fabric stiffer
- Less drape, more structure
- Shirt holds shape longer (wrinkle-resistant paradox)
- BUT: once wrinkles form, they stay (no moisture to relax)
- Static creates "crisp" quality that disappears with humidity

## HEAT FABRIC PHYSICS

**THERMAL_EXPANSION**
- Heat makes fabric fibers expand slightly
- Tight clothes become tighter (fit change visible)
- Synthetic fabric: more expansion than natural fibers
- Elastic in fabric: heat affects rebound (looser fit over time)

**SWEAT_EVIDENCE_IN_FABRIC**
```
[HEAT / EXERTION / NERVOUS SWEAT]
— underarm zone: darker wet patch spreading
— collar back: sweat accumulation (neck sweat)
— shirt front center: chest sweat pattern
— lower back: sweat band visible through shirt
— shorts: thigh sweat, groin sweat zone
```

**SWEAT_PATCH_EVIDENCE_TYPES:**
- **Active drip:** fresh sweat, dark wet zone, spreading edge
- **Saturated zone:** maximum dark, no spreading, skin shows through fabric
- **Drying zone:** ring of salt crystallization at patch edge, lighter center
- **Humid-recovered zone:** damp but not wet, color returning, salt visible

**SALT_CRYSTALLIZATION**
- Sweat evaporates, salt remains
- White crystallized edges on dark sweat patches
- Visible on dark fabrics (white salt lines)
- On light fabrics: salt as sticky residue
- Salt changes fabric texture (stiffness where sweat dried)

## RAIN FABRIC PHYSICS

### Rain Exposure Timeline

**IMMEDIATE_RAIN_CONTACT (0-2 min)**
```
— fabric surface immediately wet
— rain droplet impact visible (darker spots)
— water running down fabric (streaking pattern)
— fabric darkens from dry to wet state
— drip points at hem, cuff, collar
— water pooling on shoulders (shirt/s jacket)
```

**SATURATION_PHASE (2-10 min)**
```
— full saturation from surface to inner fabric
— weight increase visible (drooping)
— fabric fully translucent on light colors
— body outline clearly visible through fabric
— rain through shirt: skin visible, shirt clinging
— drip rate slowing as saturation reaches maximum
```

**POST-RAIN_DRIPPING (During/After rain)**
```
— water dripping from hair onto shoulders
— drips from hem (front and back)
— sleeve ends dripping
— water inside shoes visible (foot outline in puddle)
— puddle forming at feet in frame
```

**RECOVERY_PHASE (10-30 min post-rain)**
```
— surface drying begins (wind + air movement)
— fabric still wet internally but surface dry
— color lightening from wet-saturated to damp
— still heavy, still clinging, but not dripping
— flyaways activate as surface dries
— sweat mixed with rain water: different chemistry
```

## FABRIC-TYPE RESPONSE MATRIX

| Fabric Type | Humidity Response | Wet Response | Heat Response |
|-------------|------------------|--------------|---------------|
| Cotton | High absorption, softens, drapes | Heavy, dark, transparent | Absorbs sweat fast, shows patches |
| Linen | High absorption, wrinkles intensify | Heavy, droops, transparent when wet | Breathable but shows sweat |
| Polyester | Low absorption, stays dry longer | Water slides off, doesn't saturate | Sweat visible as darker patches, doesn't absorb |
| Silk | Moderate absorption, drapes heavily | Transparent when wet, clings | Shows sweat dramatically, stain visible |
| Denim | Slow absorption, stays structured | Heavy, dark, loses shape slightly | Heat trapped, sweat at waistband |
| Nylon | Water slides off, quick dry | Slight darkening, fast recovery | Shows sweat as darker zones |

## FABRIC IMPRESSION ON SKIN

**When wet fabric contacts skin and is removed:**
```
— fabric texture imprint on skin
— lines visible where fabric weave pressed into skin
— underwear lines on torso (bra strap lines, underwear waistband)
— shirt seam lines on shoulders
— collar line on neck
— duration: visible for 5-15 minutes after removal
— more visible when skin is warm (post-exercise, post-shower)
```

**This is different from pressure marks:**
- Fabric impressions: pattern matches fabric weave/texture exactly
- Pressure marks: general red line from tight elastic
- Both can occur simultaneously

## MODERATION PROFILE

### What This System Prevents:

| # | Prevent | Reason |
|---|---------|--------|
| M01 | Fabric looking dry in rain/wet scene | Physics violation — wet fabric has specific visible physics |
| M02 | Fabric color unchanged when wet | Wet fabric absorbs light — must be visibly darker |
| M03 | White shirt non-transparent when wet | Light cotton/linen wet = transparent — physics, not choice |
| M04 | Fabric floating in humid environment | Humidity causes fabric to cling, not float |
| M05 | Sweat patches not showing on fabric | Sweat is visible on fabric — underarm darkening is physics |
| M06 | Static ignored in AC scenes | AC creates floating/static behavior — must be visible |
| M07 | Post-rain fabric recovered to dry state | Water damage is cumulative — recovery takes time |
| M08 | Fabric draping unchanged in humidity | Humidity softens fabric — drape must reflect this |
| M09 | Body visible through dry fabric | Only wet/transparent fabric allows body visibility |
| M10 | Fabric not responding to weather | Fabric-first causation chain means fabric ALWAYS responds |

### Fabric Causality Moderation Rules:

1. **Fabric Always Responds:** No scene where fabric is unaffected by environment
2. **Physics-First:** Fabric behavior always follows physics before aesthetic
3. **Chain Accuracy:** Weather → Fabric → Body causation always runs in this direction
4. **Type Specificity:** Different fabric types respond differently (cotton vs polyester)
5. **Timeline Accuracy:** Fabric responds before body, visible in correct sequence
6. **Realism Over Styling:** Fabric looks like it's bearing weather weight, not styled
7. **Impression Visibility:** Wet fabric removal leaves temporary skin impressions
