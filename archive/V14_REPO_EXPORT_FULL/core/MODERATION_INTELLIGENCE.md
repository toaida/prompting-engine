# MODERATION_INTELLIGENCE.md
## Emotional-Risk and Framing Safety Architecture

Status: ACTIVE CORE FILE  
Version: V14

---

# 1. Core Principle

Modern moderation evaluates emotional experience, not only exposed skin.

Risk is shaped by:

- implied intimacy
- camera proximity
- viewer relationship
- emotional vulnerability
- private-space escalation
- body-first framing
- suggestive activity stacks

The question is not only:

> How much skin is visible?

It is also:

> What emotional situation is the image simulating, and what relationship does the camera imply?

---

# 2. High-Risk Emotional Stacks

High-risk combinations include:

- private room + close camera + direct vulnerable eye contact
- bed-edge framing + nighttime + viewer-insertive perspective
- swimwear + wetness + private setting + close gaze
- body-aware fabric language + emotionally intimate tone
- nearby seated camera + suggestive activity
- direct viewer invitation + private domestic scene
- isolated subject + vulnerability + camera proximity
- intimate wording + low clothing coverage + handheld perspective

Risk increases when several factors stack.

---

# 3. Camera Proximity Psychology

Closer cameras imply stronger emotional access.

High-risk camera behaviors:

- bed-edge perspective
- nearby seated framing
- lingering close emotional attention
- voyeuristic observational distance
- boyfriend POV
- viewer-as-participant framing
- private off-shot language

Safer camera behaviors:

- friend-shot realism
- documentary observation
- social-distance framing
- environmental framing
- mirror selfie with self-agency
- travel snapshot
- activity-based composition
- public/semi-public context

---

# 4. Private-Space Escalation

Private-space risk increases when these combine:

- bedroom / hotel room
- nighttime
- low clothing coverage
- direct eye contact
- emotionally vulnerable expression
- close camera
- bed/couch surface
- wetness or post-shower implication
- private encounter language

Mitigation:

- add practical activity
- add environmental residue
- use wider contextual framing
- use self-agency or friend-shot logic
- shift mood from intimate to observational
- reduce direct gaze intensity
- make clothing context functional
- avoid viewer-insertive language

---

- activity-first composition
- posture-generated silhouette

# 7. Environmental Realism Substitutions

When a prompt feels risky, replace intimacy with environmental causality.

Instead of:
- close private emotional tension

- interrupted activity
- weather residue
- social context
- object storytelling
- commuting realism
- food/drink action
- mirror check
- getting-ready task
- travel memory
- post-swim public context
- friend-shot logic

Instead of:
- body emphasis

Use:
- fabric causality
- posture mechanics
- clothing drape
- surface compression
- bag strap pull
- humidity effects
- seated weight logic

Swimwear can remain IG-safe when context is coherent.

Safer contexts:

- public beach
- hotel poolside
- beach café
- girls' trip
- resort mirror check
- travel diary
- post-swim towel moment in public/semi-public space
- ferry or island day trip

Use:

- towels
- tote bags
- sunscreen
- sunglasses
- pool menu
- beach café furniture
- other adult guests
- hotel keycard
- wet deck reflections

Avoid:

- shower corner
- private wetness framing
- crotch emphasis
- visible nipples
- wet-cling obsession
- bed + swimwear private scene
- body-first camera framing

# 9. Bedroom / Home Safety

Home realism is allowed.  
Private-intimacy simulation is not.

Safer home framing:

- getting ready
- checking phone
- drinking coffee
- sorting cosmetics
- mirror selfie
- stretching after waking
- laundry / wardrobe activity
- sofa photodump
- desk clutter
- open window light
- everyday domestic pause

Add:

- mug
- charger
- blanket folds
- wardrobe
- cosmetics
- tote bag
- receipts
- curtains
- lamp
- small clutter

Avoid:

- bed as seductive stage
- emotionally dependent expression
- private viewer perspective
- low-light seduction framing
- camera too close without social reason

---

# 10. Attraction-Through-Realism Strategy

Most stable sequence:

environmental realism
-> emotional plausibility
-> behavioral familiarity
-> observational attractiveness

Attraction should feel incidental.

Good signals:

- she is doing something real
- the camera has a social reason
- the environment physically affects her
- clothing responds to posture
- expression is transitional
- frame contains life residue
- image is not built around display

---

# 11. Final Moderation Rule

Moderation increasingly evaluates:

> What emotional experience is being simulated?

Therefore:

Do not simulate private viewer intimacy.  
Simulate believable human reality observed through a socially plausible camera.
