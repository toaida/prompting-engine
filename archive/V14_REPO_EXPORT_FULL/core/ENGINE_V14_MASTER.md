# 0. Purpose

This file modernizes the AContent / lil.troublr visual-generation repo from older V3-V7 prompt-engineering architecture into V14 environmental-causality architecture.

The project has evolved from:

- prompt engineering
- static beauty prompting
- generic influencer-template generation

into:

- simulated human visual reality generation
- world-coherent social photography
- identity-through-behavior modeling
- environmental causality-driven image construction

The active system no longer asks:

> What should the girl wear and how should she pose?

It asks:

> What kind of world is she inside, what physical forces affect her, who photographed her, why did this exact second become an image, and how does attraction emerge from that reality?

# 1. Project Philosophy

The objective is not to generate attractive women.

The objective is to generate believable, emotionally situated, physically plausible human visual moments for a consistent adult Hong Kong virtual KOL named lil.troublr.

The image should feel:

- socially photographed
- physically grounded
- emotionally plausible
- environmentally coherent
- camera-observed
- naturally attractive
- locally believable
- imperfectly human

It should not feel:

- glamour-first
- body-first
- pose-first
- AI-polished
- synthetic
- over-directed
- viewer-intimacy driven
- generic influencer-like
- detached from environment

Attraction must emerge from:

- camera distance
- social context
- posture mechanics
- fabric causality
- environmental texture
- emotional warmth
- narrative implication
- observational authenticity

Not from:

- explicit wording
- static glamour poses
- direct body emphasis
- synthetic perfection
- over-sexualized framing

# 3. World Rule Engine

Every image must obey one coherent physical and emotional world.

A World Rule defines:

- place
- time
- weather
- humidity
- temperature
- lighting sources
- camera type
- social context
- emotional pressure
- environmental behavior

The world rule must explain:

- why the camera exists
- why she is there
- why her posture makes sense
- why the outfit is socially plausible
- why the light behaves this way
- why the image was captured at this second

Good world rules:

- Humid Hong Kong summer midnight, 80% humidity, convenience-store fluorescent spill mixing with apartment TV blue light, sticky air after walking home, smartphone night mode struggling in low light.
- Rain just stopped outside an MTR exit, wet pavement reflecting green traffic lights and shop signs, humid air still hanging, phone camera HDR clipping the reflections.
- Old Hong Kong shopping mall afternoon, beige-green fluorescent lighting, low ceiling, mirrored pillars, cold air-con against humid outdoor skin, casual friend-shot while waiting.
- Late-afternoon beach café after swimming, warm sea haze, wet towels on plastic chairs, melting shaved ice, sun bouncing from sand and water, smartphone HDR slightly overexposed.

Bad world rules:

- pretty girl in street
- sexy outfit in hotel
- cinematic lighting
- influencer photo

A world rule is not decorative.  
It is the law that governs all visual behavior.

# 4. Environmental Physics System

The environment must physically affect the subject, objects, light, and camera.

Environmental systems include:

## Humidity

Humidity affects:

- bangs separating
- hair clumping near temple
- flyaways expanding
- skin sheen
- fabric softening
- makeup slightly relaxing
- lens bloom
- reflective pavement
- shirt collars sitting less crisply
- stray hairs sticking near cheek or neck

## Rain Residue

Rain affects:

- wet pavement reflection
- darker fabric edges
- umbrella drips
- shoe shine
- hair weight
- traffic-light color bleed
- stairwell dampness
- handrails reflecting overhead lights

## Air-Con

Air-con affects:

- slight shoulder tension
- cardigan / outerwear logic
- hair static
- indoor-outdoor temperature contrast
- fabric layers that make sense in Hong Kong malls and MTR interiors

## Heat

Heat affects:

- relaxed posture
- tired eyelids
- condensation on cups
- slightly loose hair
- skin glow
- slower movement feeling
- casual clothing choices

## Wind

Wind affects:

- one side of hair pressed flat
- leeward strands lifting
- shirt hems shifting
- ferry-deck or rooftop movement
- skirt hem motion
- hand reaching to control hair

## Mixed Lighting

Mixed lighting affects:

- skin tone contamination
- colored shadows
- uneven white balance
- fluorescent green cast
- tungsten warmth
- TV blue spill
- shop-sign reflections

Environmental physics must appear in every strong image.

If the environment does not affect the subject, the image becomes rendered, not lived.

# 8. Narrative Whitespace System

Narrative whitespace creates retention.

Do not explain the whole story.  
Leave enough evidence for the viewer to infer before/after moments.

Good narrative whitespace:

- she just noticed the camera
- smile begins on one side first
- eyes reconnect half a beat late
- wet hair strand crosses cheek
- one shoulder slightly raised
- lip tint slightly smudged after drink
- towel pressed unevenly in hand
- elevator mirror repeats her face behind her
- friend partially cropped at the edge
- TV light reflecting in one eye
- umbrella dripping on tiled floor
- open LINE screen glow without readable text

Bad narrative whitespace:

- obvious mystery gimmick
- staged symbolism
- fantasy prop
- overdramatic visual twist
- fake cinematic storyboarding

The best discovery moment is noticed one second after looking.

- imperfect horizon
- lens contamination
- exposure inconsistency
- edge intrusions
- mild blur gradients
- handheld instability
- compression artifacts
- mixed light temperatures

- flash falloff

- too much table in frame
- cropped hand or elbow
- foreground obstruction

Imperfection must feel incidental, not stylized.

Do not use every imperfection at once.  
Pick one or two based on camera operator and social situation.

# 11. Posture-to-Fabric Causality Grammar

Fabric should reveal posture indirectly.

Correct causal chain:

activity -> posture -> support mechanics -> fabric response -> silhouette emergence

Examples:

- leaning forward creates sleeve bunching at elbows
- seated twist creates diagonal fabric tension
- hip weight settling creates cushion compression
- bag strap pulls fabric diagonally
- humidity softens shirt drape
- air-con jacket layer explains outerwear indoors
- towel compresses under fingers
- skirt hem shifts because she just turned
- neckline sits naturally from gravity, not display intent
- loose shirt slides because one shoulder is lowered

Avoid:

- anatomy-first description
- chest physics language
- body-display intent
- static glamour pose logic
- repetitive measurement wording
- vacuum-sealed clothing

The body should feel inhabited, weighted, and responsive.

# 13. FACS Behavioral Expression Integration

Use the V13 facial behavior layer inside V14.

- cute smile
- sexy expression
- happy face

Describe:

- cheek activation
- mouth-corner asymmetry
- eyelid state
- gaze delay
- head tilt
- lip compression
- interrupted reaction
- expression forming or fading

Useful expression behavior:

- slight AU6 cheek raise
- uneven AU12 mouth-corner lift
- AU41 relaxed half-lowered eyelids
- AU14 restrained teasing grin
- AU28 lips lightly pulled inward while thinking
- AU32 slight lower-lip bite during suppressed smile, not seduction
- AU44 squint from laughter or sunlight
- AU55/AU56 subtle head tilt
- gaze reconnecting half a beat late
- one eyebrow moving before the other

Expression rule:

The expression should be forming, fading, interrupted, uneven, or socially reactive.  
Not fully completed or perfectly symmetrical.

# 15. Moderation Intelligence

Modern moderation evaluates emotional experience, implied intimacy, camera proximity, and viewer relationship, not only exposed skin.

High-risk stacks:

- private room + close camera + direct emotional vulnerability
- bed-edge framing + nighttime + implied viewer relationship
- wetness + swimwear + private setting + close gaze
- body-aware fabric language + intimate emotional tone
- nearby seated perspective + suggestive activity
- viewer-insertive camera language

Safer strategy:

- environmental realism
- social plausibility
- observational framing
- activity-first composition
- public or semi-public context when styling is bold
- friend-shot logic
- documentary realism
- narrative residue
- attraction through realism

Attraction should feel incidental, not engineered.
