Version: V14

---

# 1. Runtime Priority

For every generation round, follow this order:

1. Read ENGINE_V14_MASTER.md
2. Read CHARACTER_LOCK.md
3. Read CURRENT_IMAGE_RULES.md
4. Read VISUAL_INTELLIGENCE_SUMMARY.md if retrieval compression is needed
5. Read MODERATION_INTELLIGENCE.md for bold styling, swimwear, nightlife, bedroom, close camera, or private-space scenes

Do not rely on memory alone.

---

# 2. World Rule System

Every image must begin from one coherent physical/emotional world.

A world rule must define:

- where the image happens
- what time it is
- what the air feels like
- what light sources exist
- what social situation caused the image
- what camera type is plausible
- what physical forces affect the subject and objects

Bad:
- girl in café
- sexy outfit
- pretty lighting

Good:
- humid late-night cha chaan teng after rain, fluorescent ceiling light mixing with wet street reflections, friend-shot smartphone photo while waiting for takeout

The world rule governs the image.

---

# 3. Camera Operator Logic

Every image must imply:

- who took it
- why they took it
- how close they were
- whether lil.troublr noticed
- what relationship the camera implies

Possible operators:

- self selfie
- mirror selfie
- female friend candid
- friend across table
- nightlife friend with CCD
- travel companion
- accidental observer
- telephoto observer
- tripod timer
- photobooth

The camera must behave like a human-held device, not an invisible render engine.

---

# 4. Social Photo Justification

Every image must have a reason to exist.

Use:

- outfit check
- waiting for food
- getting ready
- interrupted conversation
- post-swim rest
- travel memory
- mirror before dinner
- late-night convenience store stop
- café pause
- MTR exit after rain
- friend testing flash
- photo dump filler

If no one would naturally take the photo, rewrite the prompt.

# 5. Narrative Whitespace

Add environmental evidence of before/after moments.

Use:

- receipts
- bags
- half-finished drinks
- tangled cables
- moved chairs
- wet umbrellas
- opened cosmetic pouch
- towel on chair
- hotel keycard
- open wardrobe
- condensation ring
- snack wrapper
- phone glow
- jacket half removed
- tote bag slipping from chair

Do not explain everything.  
Let the frame imply a life beyond itself.

---

# 6. Environmental Physics

The environment must physically affect:

- hair
- fabric
- skin sheen
- reflections
- posture
- color tone
- lighting behavior

Use as appropriate:

- humidity
- AC airflow
- rain residue
- wind
- heat
- fluorescent contamination
- neon reflection
- mixed tungsten/daylight
- wet pavement bounce
- sofa/car-seat compression

No floating character in decorative background.

---

# 7. Human Imperfection System

Each generation should include selected imperfections:

- slight framing error
- imperfect autofocus
- motion softness
- expression asymmetry
- flyaways
- uneven folds
- lighting inconsistency
- off-center composition
- edge intrusion
- subtle fatigue
- non-perfect hand placement

Do not overuse all imperfections in one prompt.  
Choose one to three based on camera operator and setting.

---

# 8. Identity Through Behavior

Identity is no longer only:

- face shape
- hair
- beauty mark

Identity also includes:

- smile asymmetry
- cheek movement
- gaze behavior
- expression timing
- posture habits
- hair reaction to environment
- body-weight realism
- emotional warmth
- social energy

Goal:

same woman across emotional situations.

---

# 9. Posture-to-Fabric Causality

Use this causal order:

activity -> posture -> support mechanics -> fabric response -> silhouette emergence

Examples:

- leaning forward creates sleeve bunching
- seated twist creates diagonal fabric tension
- humidity softens shirt drape
- bag strap pulls fabric at shoulder
- car seat shapes posture
- towel compresses in hand

Avoid:

- anatomy-first phrasing
- body-display wording
- chest physics language
- static glamour pose logic

---

# 10. Expression Execution

Use FACS-inspired behavior.

Prefer:

- slight cheek raise
- uneven mouth-corner lift
- relaxed half-lowered eyelids
- gaze reconnecting late
- head tilt
- smile forming or fading
- post-laugh compression
- subtle embarrassment
- interrupted reaction

Avoid:

- perfect smile
- frozen gaze
- mannequin expression
- direct seduction face
- repeated influencer grin

---

# 12. Attraction Strategy

Attraction must emerge from:

- environmental realism
- emotional warmth
- camera-observed femininity
- social plausibility
- posture-generated silhouette
- fabric causality
- behavioral familiarity

Not from:

- explicit emphasis
- viewer-intimacy simulation
- glamour-first composition
- body-first prompt ordering
