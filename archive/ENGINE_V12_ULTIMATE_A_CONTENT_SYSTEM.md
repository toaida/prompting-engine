# 7. CAMERA-SOCIAL GEOMETRY SYSTEM

## 7.1 Face Visibility

Face visibility affects social realism.

Use variation:

- full face visibility
- partial face visibility
- over-shoulder turnback
- hair-obstructed face
- phone-obstructed face
- side-profile visibility
- hidden-face candid
- mirror-face fragmentation
- face partially out of frame
- downward-facing expression

Rules:

- full face = presentation, needs activity context
- partial face = stronger social plausibility
- hidden face = observational intimacy
- hair/phone obstruction = real social behavior
- face out-of-frame = strong anti-AI cue

Avoid making every image a perfect front-facing face.

## 7.2 Eye Direction

Eye direction should imply timing and relationship.

Use:

- delayed eye contact
- eyes arriving half a beat late
- looking at friend
- looking at food/object
- distracted gaze
- off-camera observation
- mirror gaze
- downward gaze
- eyes closed during reaction
- side-eye
- post-laugh eye behavior

Avoid:

- static perfect eye contact
- dead-eye lens lock
- symmetrical mannequin gaze

Rule:

Direct eye contact is powerful but should be used selectively.

## 7.3 Emotional Timing

- peak perfect smile

Do not only describe the person.

## Gravity and Weight

Use:

- sofa cushion compresses beneath hip
- one shoulder drops from relaxed posture
- mattress folds deepen under thigh
- one knee sits higher from weight shift
- chair edge creates fabric crease
- body weight settles rather than floating
- elbow creates small indentation
- car seat subtly shapes seated posture
- towel compresses where held

## Contact Points

Identify contact:

- hip to cushion
- thigh to seat
- elbow to sofa arm
- wrist to table
- foot to floor
- shoulder to wall
- hand to cup
- fingers to phone
- hair to cheek
- robe sleeve to forearm
- towel to skin
- bag strap to shoulder

Each contact creates:

- compression
- wrinkle
- shadow
- flattening
- surface deformation
- friction bunching
- small fabric displacement

## Breathing-Affected Fabric

Use subtle cues:

- fitted cotton rises faintly with inhale
- ribcage movement creates tiny fabric tension changes
- collar shifts after exhale
- shoulder line relaxes unevenly
- neckline sits naturally rather than frozen

Keep non-fetishized.

Avoid erotic breathing language.

## Hair Micro-Climate

Use:

- damp strands cling near temple
- fringe separates from humidity
- only outer layer lifts in wind
- hair tips point differently
- strands fall across cheek
- hair near neck looks heavier from warmth
- flyaways catch side light unevenly
- rain-softened layers
- air-con static flyaways

## Incomplete Information

Use:

- slight foreground blur
- imperfect focus on one eye
- hair partially blocking cheek
- hand partly cropped
- background object intrudes at edge
- expression caught before resolving
- one detail hidden in shadow
- smartphone compression
- light clipping
