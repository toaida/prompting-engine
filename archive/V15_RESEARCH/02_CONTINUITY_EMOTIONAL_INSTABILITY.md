## AREA 03: CONTINUITY PERSISTENCE

### Core Philosophy

Recurring visual objects create the feeling of continuous life. The same bikini worn across three summers. The pendant that never leaves the neck. The tote bag with the fraying strap. The hoodie that's more lived-in than any other. Bedsheets that appear in bedroom shots across months. These objects function as **life timestamps** — evidence that this is a real person existing in real time, accumulating wear, returning to places, living a thread that the camera accidentally catches.

The mechanic is simple but powerful: **repeat objects across sessions create diegetic continuity** that no amount of staging can manufacture. When the same object appears in a 2024 photo and a 2025 photo, the viewer subconsciously reads these as connected moments in an actual life.

---

### CONTINUITY_OBJECT_LIBRARY

#### Core Principle
Every character maintains a **Personal Item Chronology** — a set of 3-7 recurring objects that appear across sessions, accumulating wear patterns, contextual associations, and minor damage that carries meaning.

---

#### Object Category A: Apparel Repeat Items

**A1. Statement Bikini (Multi-Summer Accumulation)**
- *Description:* Brand-specific bikini (top + bottom matching) that appears in pool/beach shots across 2-4 years
- *Continuity markers:*
  - Fade progression: vibrant → slightly faded → noticeably faded
  - Elastic degradation: band slightly stretched, strap slightly slack
  - Hardware: clasp dulled, underwire possibly slightly warped
  - Tanning lines: strap marks visible after first summer intensifying across years
- *Wear pattern:* Year 1 → crisp, vibrant. Year 2 → minimal fade, still supportive. Year 3 → elasticrelaxed, color mildyite diminished. Year 4 → faded, elastic clearly stretched, wire possibly poking
- *V15 prompt grammar:* `[garment]_age_accumulation_V2[2-4years]_fade_level[light/medium/heavy]_elastic_wear_[straps/band/underwire]_tanning_lines_from_previous_sessions`
- *Example:* A striped bikini appearing in 2023, 2024, and 2025 beach photos — slight color fading readable as chronological accumulation

**A2. Signature Necklace / Pendant**
- *Description:* Chain or pendant necklace worn consistently, rarely removed, accumulates skin oils and micro-scratches
- *Continuity markers:*
  - Metal oxidation: bright → dulled → potentially slight green tarnish at connection points
  - Chain links: subtle kinks or twists develop from repeated wear
  - Clasp: slightly worn, possibly easiest to open now vs. original tightness
  - Skin contact: chain slightly stained where it contacts skin lotion/sweat
- *V15 prompt grammar:* `[metal_type]_[pendant_style]_necklace_wear_[new/slightly_worn/worn]_skin_oil_accumulation_chain_[flexible/slightly_rigid]_clasp_[tight/sluggish]`
- *Example:* A silver pendant necklace worn every day for 2+ years develops patina and chain flexibility changes

**A3. The Emotional Hoodie**
- *Description:* Favorite hoodie worn repeatedly at home, lounging, casual outings — appears in multiple informal sessions
- *Continuity markers:*
  - Pilling: fabric pills in high-friction areas (cuffs, pocket edges, hem)
  - Color degradation: particularly at elbows and cuffs where arm flexion causes stress
  - Shape memory: hoodie has molded slightly to body shape from repeated wear
  - Lint accumulation: particularly around pocket and hem
  - Zipper: slider slightly stiff if metallic, teeth may have shifted alignment
- *V15 prompt grammar:* `[color]_hoodie_repeat_wear_fabric_pilling_[cuffs/pockets/hem/all]_color_fade_at_[stress_points]_shape_molded_to_body_zipper_[smooth/stiff]`
- *Example:* A gray supremel-branded hoodie appearing in multiple home/lifestyle shots over 18 months shows progressive pilling at wrist cuffs

**A4. Rotational Tote Bag**
- *Description:* Canvas or leather tote used repeatedly for shopping/daily errands/weekend beach trips
- *Continuity markers:*
  - Handle wear: one handle more worn if consistently held same hand, rope-style handles show fiber fraying
  - Body shape: tote loses structure slightly, bottom may show stress, corners show rub wear
  - Interior: lining may show pencil/pen marks, lip gloss exploded in bottom corner, old receipts tissue fragments
  - Hardware: metal studs/feet on bottom dulled/scratched from floor contact
- *V15 prompt grammar:* `[material]_tote_repeat_wear_handle_[single_hand_bias/symmetric]_body_shape_[structured/slightly_sagged]_corner_rub_[mild/noticeable]_interior_contents_[detail_level]`
- *Example:* A canvas tote bag showing darker hand-corner wear pattern from repeated single-hand grip

**A5. Specific Underwear Set (Visible Through Outfit)**
- *Description:* Specific bra/panties set visible at edges when wearing low-back, sheer, or tight clothing
- *Continuity markers:*
  - Slight elastic degradation: band less supportive, straps slightly widened
  - Color: slight migration/staining from where skin meets fabric daily
  - Fabric texture: slightly more lived-in texture rather than new crispness
  - Hook-and-eye: slightly more worn at closure points

### RECURRING-OBJECT ARCHITECTURE

#### System A: Object-Time Mapping

**Core mechanic:** Each recurring object carries a **Temporal Signature** — the combination of wear, fading, and damage that allows a viewer to subconsciously place the object on a timeline.

```
Object State Tiers:
├── TIER_0: NEW / FRESHLY ACQUIRED
│   └── Object appears crisp, no wear, tags possibly visible
├── TIER_1: 3-6 MONTHS REGULAR USE
│   └── New-product feel gone, early wear visible
├── TIER_2: 1 YEAR REGULAR USE
│   └── Full break-in, meaningful wear, character mold developing
├── TIER_3: 2+ YEARS REGULAR USE
│   └── Visible degradation, possibly seeking replacement soon
└── TIER_4: VINTAGE / SENTIMENTAL
    └── Object is old but maintained past normal replacement point
```

**Moderation Profile:**
- Each character should have 3-7 persistent items across any single recurring object category
- No more than 2 items should be TIER_0 in use at once — otherwise character feels new-having
- TIER_3+ items should have narrative explanation (sentimental attachment, financial constraint, brand loyalty)
- Correlated wear: objects worn together should age together (sneakers + socks, necklace + earrings)

---

#### System B: Object-Environment Affinity

**Core mechanic:** Certain objects **belong** in specific environments and their reappearance in those environments creates continuity. Object in context = life happening in location.

| Object | Primary Environment | Reappearance Context |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Same bikini | Beach/pool | Every summer, same beach location |
| Same tote bag | Street/shopping | Regular shopping streets, wet market visits |
| Same hoodie | Home/indoor | Lounging, lazy Sunday, pre-sleep |
| Same water bottle | Gym/street | Post-workout, mid-street walking |
| Same bedding | Bedroom | Morning light, night, lazy weekend |

**Continuity type distinction:**
- **Diegetic continuity:** Same object appearing in same diegetic world across sessions (same character, same physical world)
- **Temporal continuity:** Object wear progression readable across time (bikini getting more faded = time passed)
- **Emotional continuity:** Object carries sentimental weight that viewer can read (obsolete phone case on newest phone = "keeps the case for sentimental reasons")

---

#### System C: Object Accumulation Logic

**Layer principle:** Each object has three narrative layers:

```
Layer 1: Visual Continuity
└── Same object type/brand visible across sessions

Layer 2: Wear Progression
└── Object accumulates wear damage consistent with timeline

Layer 3: Behavioral Affinity
└── Object is consistently involved in same activity types
```

**Example — Same Bikini across 3 summers:**
1. Summer 1: "bikini_appears_in_beach_photo_first_wear_vibrant_color_no_fade"
2. Summer 2: "same_bikini_summer_2_mild_fade_elastic_slightly_relaxed_tan_lines_visible"
3. Summer 3: "same_bikini_multiple_washes_fade_progression_elastic_relaxed_underwire_slightly_warped

### OUTFIT-REPEAT SYSTEM

#### Core Philosophy
Outfits repeated across sessions (same dress, same shirt) create **life routine continuity** — the viewer reads "this person has a rotation, this isn't a one-time set dressed for the camera."

#### System A: Rotation Frequency Model

**Moderation profile by repetition type:**

| Outfit Type | Optimal Repeat Cadence | Maximum Across Dataset | Wear Progression Rate |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Statement piece (bikini, event dress) | 2-4x per year | 3-4 years | Slow (accumulating) |
| Daily wear (hoodie, tee, sneaks) | 15-30x per year | Continuously replenished | Fast (obvious after 6 months) |
| Special occasion (wedding, birthday) | 1x per year maximum | 5+ years | Essentially none (worn once) |
| Active/sports (gym wear, swimsuit) | 3-5x per month | 1-2 years | Fast (sweat/salt accelerated) |

**Key principle:** The ratio of "times worn" to "times photographed" should skew heavily toward times worn. A bikini worn 50 times but photographed 3 times across 2 years = high realism.

#### System B: Outfit-State Tracking

**State variance for repeated items:**
- *SAME OUTFIT (immediate repeat):* Worn within 48 hours, minimal laundering difference, same level of "freshness"
- *SAME OUTFIT (contextual repeat):* Worn again 2-4 weeks later, washed once, wrinkles from storage visible
- *SAME ITEM, DIFFERENT COMPLETION:* Same top paired with different bottom/footwear = outfit variation from same garment pool
- *SAME ITEM, WEAR PROGRESSION:* Same bikini in Year 2 vs Year 3 = visible age difference

#### System C: V15-Prompt Grammar for Outfit Repeats

```
SAME_ITEM_REPEAT:
[item_description]_worn_[frequency_estimation]_wash_cycle_[number]_wrinkle_storage_pattern_[level]_item_age_[tier]

NEW_FROM_WARDROBE:
[item_description]_first_wear_tag_[visible/not_visible]_store_starch_[present/absent]_size_label_[brand_size]_[vintage_era]

COMPLETE_OUTFIT_REPEAT:
[full_outfit_description]_all_items_from_same_session_[date/occasion]_vs_[new items from different session]
```

### TRAVEL CONTINUITY MODULE

#### Core Philosophy
Travel creates a specific continuity challenge: objects appearing in foreign environments are simultaneously **location evidence** and **personal identity anchors**. A Hong Kong character's tote bag appearing in a Japan photo creates geographic range continuity.

#### System A: Travel Object Types

**Type 1: Packed-Luggage Items**
- Items character brings from home to destination
- Carry recognizable personal objects to foreign context
- Function as **identity anchors** in unfamiliar environment
- Examples: Same tote bag, same water bottle, same phone case

**Type 2: Destination-Appropriate Items**
- New purchases or items that only appear in travel context
- Function as **location markers** (tourist购买的街头小吃)
- Should be visually differentiated from character's Hong Kong wardrobe
- Examples: Local brandT恤 purchased abroad, local bag brand acquired on trip

**Type 3: Travel-Specific Items**
- Items that only exist in travel context (passport, travel adapter, foreign currency)
- Function as **departure/arrival signals**
- Appear in departure shots and return-shots, absent during normal domestic sessions

#### System B: Cross-Location Object Rules

**Rule 1: No object can appear in more than 2 dramatically different environment types without narrative explanation**
- Exception: Household objects (phone case, jewelry) ARE allowed in all environments
- Rule: Tote bag at HK street + Japan temple + Bali beach = allowed (versatile character item)
- Rule: Hotel bathroom slippers appearing in HK apartment = discontinuity error

**Rule 2: Travel wear progression is compressed**
- 1-week trip may show same item in 3-5 contexts = accelerated contextual wear
- Beach trip items should show sand/salt/sun dramatically faster than domestic equivalents

**Rule 3: Destination-acquired items should accumulate wear independently**
- New Japan jacket gets worn only in Japan context for that trip
- If character wears it back in HK 6 months later, it should show its own independent wear progression

#### System C: V15-Prompt Grammar for Travel Continuity

```
TRAVEL_OBJECT_PRESENT:
[item_description]_carried_to_[destination]_from_[origin]_destination_context_[tourist/local/off-duty]_

DESTINATION_PURCHASE:
[new_item]_acquired_at_[specific_location]_first_wear_[destination]_return_wear_[origin_after_trip]_

DEPARTURE_EVIDENCE:
passport_[character_name]_travel_[destination]_departure_date_[implied]_carry-on_items_[list]
```

## AREA 04: EMOTIONAL INSTABILITY

### Core Philosophy

Human emotional expression is never perfectly timed. We don't start smiling the instant something becomes funny. Our laughter doesn't stop cleanly when the moment ends. Our expressions lag, anticipate, interrupt, and trail off. The smile isn't finished before the camera catches it. The laugh catches mid-breath. The sleepy face and the joyful face exist in the same frame at different activation levels.

Emotional Instability is the **temporal imperfection of internal state expression**. It is the opposite of the "ready for the camera" expression that makes people look like they're performing happiness rather than experiencing it.

**Key distinction from AREA 01 (Camera Awareness):**
- AREA 01 is about whether the subject knows they're being photographed
- AREA 04 is about whether the subject's emotional expression is fully formed and complete
- A subject can be unaware of the camera (noise) but still have a finished, composed expression
- A subject can be aware of the camera but still have an interrupted, incomplete emotional response

---

### EXPRESSION_TIMING_LIBRARY_V2

#### Core Principle
Every emotional expression exists on a **timing spectrum** from **anticipation** → **activation** → **peak** → **dissolution** → **residual**. The most emotionally real photographs capture expressions at non-peak points.

---

#### Expression Category A: Joy-Laughter Spectrum

**A1. Laugh Onset (Anticipation Phase)**
- *Description:* The moment right before laughter becomes audible — the face changes before the sound
- *Visual markers:*
  - Eye change first: Orbicularis oculi activation (eye muscles) BEFORE mouth opens
  - Nasolabial furrows deepen immediately before laugh sound
  - Shoulders begin upward movement 100-200ms before vocalization
  - Startle response: strong apex laughter begins with shoulder jerk + body jolt
- *Duration:* 150-400ms from first eye contraction to full mouth open
- *Realism trigger:* Photographing just before audible laugh = the face is already laughing but not "performing laughter yet"
- *V15 prompt grammar:* `laughs_onset_contraction_[upper_face/lower_face/both]_timing_[pre_sound/post_sound]_shoulder_anticipation_[yes/no]`

**A2. Laugh Peak Hold (Overextended)**
- *Description:* Person laughing continues laughing slightly past the moment the joke ended — laughter was funnier than expected, overshot the natural endpoint
- *Visual markers:*
  - Laughter continuing 500-1000ms longer than social script would predict
  - Slight head tilt back maintained during extended tail
  - Hands may be raised toward face (covering mouth mid-laugh)
  - Breath intake visible at end — sudden inhalation mid-resolution
- *Duration:* 400-1200ms beyond expected endpoint
- *Realism trigger:* Captured at natural comedown point rather than peak = observer reads as "caught in actual joy, not posing"

**A3. Laugh Dissolution (Interruption/V trail-off)**
- *Description:* Laughter ending not cleanly but trailing off — person tries to stop but something triggers another mini-burst
- *Visual markers:*
  - Mouth position changes: open laugh → teeth show → tongue visible → [moment] → slight reopening
  - Breath pattern: exhale-laugh → sudden cut → short inhale → weak second exhale
  - Residual eye crinkling: Orbicularis oculi stays partially active 500ms+ after mouth closes
  - Shoulder lowering not synchronized: one shoulder drops before other
- *Duration:* 800-2000ms total dissolution time
- *Realism trigger:* The reformation of the smile after apparent conclusion signals "genuine emotional overflow"

**A4. Smiles That Don't Finish**
- *Description:* Smile begins but doesn't complete — mouth opens slightly, then partially closes as subject redirects attention before completing expression
- *Visual markers:*
  - Mouth opens to "o" position briefly → jaw returns halfway → expression incomplete
  - One side of mouth engages before other: asymmetric onset
  - Orbicularis oculi: NO eye involvement = signals social smile vs. genuine humor smile
  - Eyebrow position: neutral-to-slight-raise rather than inner-brow raise (Duchenne marker absent)
- *Realism trigger:* Unfinished expressions read as "disrupted mid-state, caught in cognitive redirection"

**A5. Sleepy Joy (Low-Arousal Positive)**
- *Description:* Person is tired/sleepy but finds something genuinely funny — high-arousal emotion (joy) combining with low-arousal physical state (fatigue)
- *Visual markers:*
  - Eye half-open: lids at 40-60% closure during attempted full eye engagement laugh
  - Delayed response: laughter starting 500-800ms after stimulus (processing slowdown from fatigue)
  - Facial flush: slight redness in cheeks from effort of staying present
  - Head nod forward: head heavy, jaw possibly dropping slightly mid-laugh from tiredness
- *Key aesthetic:* The contrast between the strength of the humor response and the physical limitation of being tired creates emotional complexity

---

#### Expression Category B: Startle-Surprise States

**B1. Pre-Peak Anticipatory Tension**
- *Description:* Person registering surprise but not yet at peak expression — face in preparation/stabilization before full response
- *Visual markers:*
  - Eyebrows: rising but not yet fully elevated
  - Eyes: still engaged, not yet widened
  - Hands: possibly in motion toward face, positioned at chest level
  - Body: leaning toward rather than fully committed
- *Timing:* 50-150ms after surprising stimulus, before peak reaction
- *Realism trigger:* PrePeak = "about to react but hasn't yet"

**B2. Peak Reaction Hold (Overexaggeration)**
- *Description:* Full surprise reaction held slightly beyond natural duration — social performance of surprise layering on genuine surprise
- *Visual markers:*
  - Full eyebrow elevation sustained
  - Hand(s) fully to face
  - Slight backward lean if surprise is large
  - Mouth: "O" position fully open, possible vocalization occurring
- *Timing:* Reaction kept 300-600ms past point where natural response would de-escalate
- *Distinction:* Genuine surprise peaks and descends within 400ms, overexaggerated holds past 600ms

**B3. Surprise Dissolution (Confusion Integration)**
- *Description:* Person processing surprising information — surprise blending with attempt to understand/respond
- *Visual markers:*
  - Eyebrows lowering but not fully: processing mode engaged
  - Eye gaze: may have shifted to look at source of surprise
  - Head tilt: slight lateral tilt as brain works to integrate information
  - Mouth: jaw moderately open, possible slight forward thrust as seeking words

---

#### Expression Category C: Fatigue-Sleep States

**C1. Sleepy Drowsiness (Not Yet Asleep)**
- *Description:* Person approaching sleep but not yet there — eyes heavy, awareness fluctuating
- *Visual markers:*
  - Eyelids: at 30-50% closure, drooping
  - Blink duration: single blinks lasting 400-700ms (normal blink is 100-200ms)
  - Head: forward droop developing, chin moving toward chest
  - Posture: body sinking into support, weight increasing on whatever is bearing load
  - Pupils: possibly slightly dilated from reduced lighting, small movements tracking slowly
- *Realism trigger:* Mid-drool if jaw slack, eyes not tracking normally

**C2. Micro-Sleep Fragment (Involuntary)**
- *Description:* Person briefly loses awareness and enters sleep state for seconds — not voluntary rest
- *Visual markers:*
  - Sudden head drop: chin-to-chest slam
  - Eyelid flutter: rapid partial open-close
  - Body jerk: myoclonic jerk at moment of sleep onset
  - Posture collapse: body weight suddenly transfers to support as muscles relax
  - Recovery: startled reawakening 2-5 seconds later
- *Duration:* 2-10 seconds microsleep episode
- *Risk:* Camera misses eye closure — eyes may be photographed partially open during microsleep

**C3. Fatigue Pain (The Tired Face)**
- *Description:* Accumulated fatigue showing on face — person tired but functioning, not microsleeping
- *Visual markers:*
  - Malar flush: redness concentrated on cheekbones from tired blood vessel dilation
  - Under-eye: slight puffiness or darkened circles
  - Posture: shoulders elevated and forward, skull compensating with forward head posture
  - Mouth: slightly open to facilitate breathing with reduced respiratory drive
- *Key variant:* "Crying tired" — fatigue combining with emotional overwhelm, slightly reddened eyes

---

#### Expression Category D: Distraction-Interruption States

**D1. Mid-Sentence Interruption**
- *Realism trigger:* Person in conversation, photographed while talking, expression interrupted mid-thought
- *Visual markers:*
  - Mouth still in position for recently finished word
  - Eyes: gaze not synchronized with mouth — eyes looking at something else mouth wasn't addressed to
  - Eyebrows: in expression position appropriate to thought being hold BEFORE full interruption registered
  - Hands: possibly mid-gesture, frozen in air
- *Key characteristic:* Face occupying two emotional positions simultaneously: what was being expressed before + what is being registered after interruption

**D2. Attention Migration (Partially There)**
- *Description:* Person's attention divided — part of face engaged with camera/subject, part engaged with something else
- *Visual markers:*
  - Eye gaze: looking at camera but focus seems to be "through" camera at something else
  - Mouth: slight smile (engaged with camera) but eyes not reciprocating
  - Eyebrows: one raised to attentional position, one neutral from divided attention
  - Body: shoulders angled back toward distraction source while torso faces camera
- *Distinction from AREA 01: Camera-blindness:* Divided attention involves split engagement with ENVIRONMENT, not awareness of camera

**D3. Environment Scanning**
- *Description:* Person looking around the environment mid-expression — attention still in scene but eyes moving
- *Visual markers:*
  - Expression partially maintained while eyes sweep: smile present but not eye-supported
  - Micro-expressions: corner of mouth shifting slightly as gaze changes emotional context
  - Body stays oriented toward primary target; eyes conduct lateral search
  - Brief eyebrow raise when something in environment catches attention — then returns to original expression

---

#### Expression Category E: Cognitive Load Expressions

**E1. Thinking Face (Mid-Process)**
- *Description:* Person working through a thought — not yet reached conclusion or response formulation
- *Visual markers:*
  - Gaze: upward and lateral (classic "looking for memory/searching for words" position)
  - Eyebrows: slight inward contraction (Corrugator activation) — "working" expression
  - Mouth: lips pressed together or slight protrusion ("pouting" while thinking)
  - Head: slight lateral tilt, axis rotation toward preferred processing side (usually right)
  - Hand-to-face: possibly touching chin/cheek as support for thinking posture
- *Duration variability:* Thinking face can last 2-30 seconds depending on cognitive task complexity

**E2. Thought Interruption (Just Got Idea)**
- *Description:* Person mid-thought, idea strikes — thought pathway illuminated suddenly
- *Visual markers:*
  - Sudden eyebrow raise: inner brow raiser (frontalis) before eyes fully engage with new idea
  - Eye roll-up: not lateral, but straight up — "接受了"
  - Mouth: small "o" or lips parted — "oh"
  - Hand gesture possibly frozen mid-air as idea consumed full attention before gesture could complete
  - Brief flash of recognition before full engagement

---

#### Expression Category F: Social Inhibition Expressions

**F1. Suppressed Laughter**
- *Description:* Something is funny but social context requires suppression — smile or laugh held back
- *Visual markers:*
  - Orbicularis oculi activation WITHOUT full mouth engagement: crow's feet present, mouth just slightly upturned
  - Head turn or duck: physical redirection away from humor source
  - Hand to mouth: covering partial smile, palm on upper lip
  - Shoulder elevation: physical suppression sign — shrug-into-self
  - Breath: held briefly, released in controlled exhale after humor passes
- *Distinction from genuine neutral:* The SUPRACONTRACTION of eye muscle tells the viewer "this person is suppressing positive emotion"

**F2. Controlled/Uncontrolled Expression Asymmetry**
- *Description:* One side of face expressing emotional state while other side maintains control/social norm
- *Visual markers:*
  - Asymmetric smile: one corner more elevated than other
  - One eyebrow raised while other remains neutral
  - One eye engaged while other appears neutral-dominant
- *Social function:* Social self vs. genuine self split visible in face

---

#### Expression Category G: Residual Expression States

**G1. Post-Emotion Residual Muscle Activation**
- *Description:* Emotional expression complete but facial muscles not fully returned to neutral
- *Visual markers:*
  - Nasolabial folds: still slightly deepened 1-3 seconds after smile resolves
  - Eye corner creasing: residual crow's feet visible after eyes re-open
  - Mouth corner: still slight upward turn for several seconds
  - Eyebrow position: may not fully return to baseline immediately
- *Realism trigger:* Residual activation = "this expression was real and just ended"

**G2. Emotion-Contaminated Neutral**
- *Description:* Face attempting to return to neutral but emotion bleeds through
- *Visual markers:*
  - Slight mouth corner elevation: neutral face with residual smile
  - Brow position: almost neutral but slight inner-brow elevation suggests something was funny recently
  - Eye brightness: residual eye-lighting from recent positive emotion

### EMOTIONAL-INSTABILITY FRAMEWORK

#### System A: Expression Completeness Spectrum

Every expression can be rated on a **completeness scale**:

```
COMPLETENESS LEVELS:
├── LEVEL 0: NO EXPRESSION (neutral baseline)
├── LEVEL 1: ANTICIPATORY (expression beginning but not visible externally)
├── LEVEL 2: ONSET (micro-expression visible — under 200ms into expression)
├── LEVEL 3: EMERGING (full expression beginning to claim face)
├── LEVEL 4: PEAK (fully formed expression)
├── LEVEL 5: POST-PEAK (expression dissolving, residual activation)
└── LEVEL 6: RETURNING (almost neutral but emotion contamination visible)
```

**Moderation profile:** High-realism photographs over-index on LEVEL 2 (emerging onset) and LEVEL 5-6 (dissolution/residual) compared to posed photography which centers on LEVEL 4 (peak, composed).

#### System B: Expression Duration Mapping

| Expression Type | Typical Peak Duration | Dissolution Duration | "Too Long" Threshold |
|-----------------|---------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Genuine smile | 400-800ms | 200-400ms | Over 1200ms total |
| Social smile | 300-600ms | 100-300ms | Can appear briefly |
| Laughter | 500-1500ms | 500-2000ms | Over 2500ms |
| Surprise | 200-400ms | 300-500ms | Over 800ms |
| Sleepy | Continuous | N/A | Can last subject's full session |

#### System C: Interruption-Timing Categories

**Interrupt types by cause:**
- **EXTERNAL INTERRUPTION:** Something in environment interrupts emotional expression (sound, visual event, third party entering frame)
- **COGNITIVE INTERRUPTION:** Subject's own thought process interrupts emotion (idea strikes, attention migrates)
- **SELF-INHIBITION:** Subject deliberately suppresses expression (social calibration, shyness, self-consciousness)
- **ENDOGENOUS INTERRUPTION:** Emotion peaks and self-corrects naturally (reaction completion without external cause)

**Interruption moment categories:**
- **Pre-expression:** Stimulus occurs but expression never completes
- **Mid-expression:** Expression begins and is interrupted before peak
- **Peak-clipped:** Expression reaches peak but camera misses duration
- **Post-peak:** Camera catches dissolution, missing the actual peak

### UNFINISHED-EXPRESSION SYSTEM

#### Core Principle
The **unfinished expression** is the single most powerful emotional realism tool. An expression that doesn't complete tells the viewer "this person was interrupted, distracted, or couldn't complete their emotional response" — which is true to how humans actually move through emotional states.

#### System A: Five Unfinished Types

**TYPE 1: THE CUT-OFF SMILE**
- *What is missing:* The smile onset is present but mouth doesn't fully engage
- *Visual form:* Mouth opens slightly, then freezes rather than drawing back into full smile
- *Reader interpretation:* "Something interrupted before the smile could complete — caught mid-adjustment"
- *Prompt grammar:* `smile_onset_mouth_[slightly_open/freeze_point]_before_full_engagement_orbicularis_[incomplete_activation]_expression_cut`

**TYPE 2: THE HELD LAUGH**
- *What is missing:* Laugh begins but is held back internally — physical laughter suppressed
- *Visual form:* Eye engagement present (orbicularis oculi), mouth just slightly upturned but not open, breath held
- *Reader interpretation:* "Something was very funny but subject chose not to fully laugh — social calibration moment"
- *Prompt grammar:* `genuine_laugh_suppressed_hand_to_mouth_breath_held_orbicularis_[fully_active/incomplete]_mouth_[slight_smile/plain]_shoulder_[elevated/neutral]`

**TYPE 3: THE FADING EXPRESSION**
- *What is missing:* Expression when captured is already dissolving — peak was missed
- *Visual form:* Nasolabial folds partially deepened, residual eye crinkling, mouth at partial return toward neutral
- *Reader interpretation:* "This expression was real and already passing when captured — moment already happened"
- *Prompt grammar:* `expression_fading_[smile/laugh/surprise]_residual_[nasolabial/crowfeet/mouth_corner]_activation_[number]ms_post_peak_[level]`

**TYPE 4: THE MISALIGNED EXPRESSION**
- *What is missing:* Face showing simultaneous contradictory emotional positions — not synchronized
- *Visual form:* Eyes showing one emotion (attention to something in environment) while mouth shows different (engagement with camera/person)
- *Reader interpretation:* "Subject's attention and expression are divided — caught between two emotional states"
- *Prompt grammar:* `divided_attention_face_eye_[looking_at/thwarting]_mouth_[smile/neutral/speaking]_asymmetry_[emotion_1]_[emotion_2]_[simultaneous_contradiction]`

**TYPE 5: THE STATED-THEN-INTERRUPTED EXPRESSION**
- *What is missing:* Expression visible from earlier emotional event lingers, but current state is different
- *Visual form:* Residual Duchenne markers (eye crinkling) plus current neutral or negative state creating timeline confusion on face
- *Reader interpretation:* "This face was just laughing/was just upset — caught in the transition back to neutral"
- *Prompt grammar:* `post_expression_residual_[smile/frown/surprise]_current_state_[neutral/shifting]_transition_[ongoing]_muscle_return_rate_[slow/medium/fast]`

#### System C: Moderation Profile — Expression Instability

**Type distribution target:**
- **50%** should be at non-peak timing (anticipation, onset, or dissolution)
- **30%** should be peak expressions (natural peak timing, not overexaggerated)
- **15%** should be interrupted or incomplete
- **5%** can be overexaggerated (for specific character types: theatrical personalities, performers)

**Instability type distribution:**
- Sleepy-joy combos: 15-20% of emotional instability captures (high visual interest)
- Laugh interruption: 20-25% (most common, most naturally occurring)
- Distracted mid-expression: 20-25% (second most common)
- Cognitive interruption: 10-15% (thinking face variants)
- Residual expression: 15-20% (post-emotion captures)

**Off-limits / moderation warnings:**
- Do not capture subjects in genuine distress (fear, rage at high intensity) without model sensitivity protocols
- Fatigue expressions should NOT show signs of medical distress (snoring, severe pallor)
- Emotional instability should be COMICAL/COMPANIONABLE for lil.troublr context, not clinical/medical
- Laughing-at-nothing vs laughing-at-something: the joke stimulus should be socially legible

#### System D: Behavioral Mechanics — Expression Imperfection Triggers

**Trigger 1: The Timing Gap**
When subject experiences something funny/interesting but camera captures them slightly before or after the response:
- Pre-response: eye contraction beginning but mouth still neutral
- Post-response: mouth returning to neutral but eyes still engaged

**Trigger 2: The Override**
When subject is consciously managing their expression but body/mind briefly betrays the management:
- Suppressed laugh: eyes engaging while mouth purposefully held neutral
- Social smile: mouth engaged but eyes not reciprocating

**Trigger 3: The Double-Booking**
When two emotional states are simultaneously active (attention divided):
- Eyes tracking environment while mouth is socially engaged with primary subject
- Thinking face combining with attempted smile — brain working while socially performing

**Trigger 4: The Residual Echo**
When emotional expression has physically resolved but face hasn't fully caught up:
- Micro-expression remnants visible 1-5 seconds after emotional event complete
- Natural post-laugh lingering: eyes still bright, residual nasolabial activation

**Trigger 5: The Sleepy-Arousal Mismatch**
When fatigue creates delay between stimulus and response OR reduces expression amplitude:
- Too tired to maintain full laugh
- Too sleepy to complete the smile
- Too exhausted to lift eyebrows fully in response to surprise

## BEHAVIORAL MECHANICS: CONTINUITY + INSTABILITY INTEGRATION

### Combined System Principle

Continuity objects and emotional instability are **orthogonal dimensions** that weave together to create life-feel:

- **Continuity objects:** "this is the same person across time" (horizontal life thread)
- **Emotional instability:** "this person is in the middle of an unposed emotional moment" (vertical authenticity spike)

When combined, the impression is: **"this real person, who has lived real time, is currently mid-feeling something they haven't finished processing"** — which is the most emotionally compelling photographic state.
