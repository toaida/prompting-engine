# 1. FACE VISIBILITY SYSTEM

Core Principle:
Full face = presentation.
Partial face = social realism.
Hidden face = observational intimacy.

Preferred V11 face states:
- over-shoulder turnback
- partial obstruction by hair
- side-profile visibility
- mirror fragmentation
- phone-obstructed selfie
- face partly outside frame
- downward-facing expressions

Anti-AI mechanisms:
- asymmetric face visibility
- incomplete facial exposure
- hair crossing one eye
- face partially hidden by objects
- face not centered
- face interrupted by activity

Behavior rules:
- avoid static frontal face posing
- avoid perfect bilateral symmetry
- avoid centered beauty framing
- allow awkwardness
- allow incomplete face exposure
- allow movement interruption

--------------------------------------------------
# 2. EYE DIRECTION SYSTEM

Core Principle:
Held eye contact feels posed.
Delayed gaze feels alive.

Preferred V11 eye states:
- eyes arriving late to camera

- distracted gaze
- eyes on object/food/phone
- off-camera observation
- post-laugh unfocused eyes
- downward gaze
- side-eye
- mirror self-observation

Anti-AI mechanisms:
- incomplete eye contact
- gaze transition
- blink irregularity
- emotional eye lag
- eyes reacting instead of presenting

Behavior rules:
- avoid static direct gaze
- avoid fully symmetrical eyes
- avoid frozen eye contact
- prioritize "caught noticing camera"
- prioritize transitional gaze
